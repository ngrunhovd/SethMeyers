Seth Meyers was born December 28, 1973; he is known as a comedian, writer, actor, producer, former SNL cast member, and host of Late Night with Seth Meyers on NBC since February 2014. He is married with 3 children and is an avid fan of Boston and Pittsburgh sports teams.

Career

Meyers graduated from Northwestern University in his hometown of Evanston, IL in 1996 with a Bachelor's in Film & Television Production. He credits his comedy influences to Monty Python, Seinfeld, David Letterman, Conan O'Brien, and Dennis Miller. He began his career in improv comedy and performed in several groups until 2001 when he joined the cast of Saturday Night Live.

During his time on SNL, Seth received high praise for his writing and impressions. After fellow cast member Tina Fey left SNL in 2006, he was promoted to head writer and co-host of SNL Weekend Update with Amy Poehler. Meyers left SNL in 2014 and landed the gig as the new host of Late Night following Jimmy Fallon's move to host The Tonight Show.

In the first 8 years that Late Night with Seth Meyers has been on air, it has been nominated for a Primetime Emmy 9 times but has no wins currently. The nominations were four times for Outstanding Writing, twice for Outstanding Directing, one time for Outstanding Variety Talk Series, and twice in the Outstanding Short Form Comedy category for his recurring "Corrections" segment streaming on YouTube.

In addition to late night TV hosting, Meyers has hosted many awards shows and built his IMDB credits page as a writer, producer, actor, voice actor, and even became a published author for the juvenile fiction book "I'm Not Scared, You're Scared". More recently his production company, Sethmaker Shoemeyers, was behind hit shows A.P. Bio, The Amber Ruffin Show, and Mapleworth Murders; with more projects in various stages of development.

Personal Life

Seth Meyers was born in Illinois, spent his early child-hood in Michigan, and then grew up in Bedford, New Hampshire beginning at the age of 10. His mother was a French teacher and father worked in finance. He has one younger brother, Josh Meyers, who is also an actor most notable for his time on MadTV and as the character Randy on That 70's Show. Seth has been married to his wife Alexi Ashe since 2013 and the two have 3 children together.

Having grown up in the New England area, he is a fan of the Boston Red Sox and Boston Celtics. However, the rest of his fan allegience lies with Pittsburgh along with father's favorites; the Pittsburgh Steelers, Pittsburgh Pirates, and Pittsburgh Penguins. He has also been known to be a supporter of two soccer teams; the Netherlands national team and West Ham United of the Premiere League.
