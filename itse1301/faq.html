What the heck is a jackal?
A jackal is a type of canine related to dogs and coyotes. Native to Africa and Eurasia, it has coloring like a German Shepherd and a build like a tall, slender fox with a fluffy tail. It's also a term used to describe a devoted group of Late Night fans that like to point out mistakes made on the show. This lead to the creation of the Emmy-nominated segment Corrections, in which Seth acknowledges these jackals contributions and their absurdity.

Does Seth Meyers have any other projects?

Has Seth Meyers appeared in any movies?
Yes, he has over 30 acting credits including several movie and TV shows. His first movie appearance was an uncredited role in "Village of the Damned" in 1995. He has done many cameo appearances in movies playing characters or versions of himself. His role in the movie, "New Year's Eve" earned a Razzie nomination as part of Worst Screen Ensemble in a movie... along with the rest of the cast.

What is Seth Meyers the author of?
He first co-authored the comic issue of "Spider-Man: The Short Halloween" in 2009 with fellow SNL star, Bill Hader. He also published a funny children's picture book in 2022 called "I'm Not Scared, You're Scared", about a bear and his rabbit friend.
